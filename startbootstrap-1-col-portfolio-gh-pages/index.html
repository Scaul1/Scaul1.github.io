Sydney Caulfeild Portfolio

Hi! My name is Sydney Caulfeild and I am second-year engineering student at Queen's University. I have been studying computer science for three years and will be entering computer engineering in the fall. While in 12th grade I worked on several projects and courses on Khan Academy. The top projects are described below.

1. Shiffman Coding Challenge

This project is a replication of Dan Shiffman's Flappy Bird Coding Challenge. He created a YouTube video where he codes a challenge live and I coded along with him to learn the code. I then added to his game by creating a bird image, modifying the gravity and lift variables and increasing the speed of the pipes as they approach the bird to make it more challenging. This video demonstrates how the game works: the user presses the space bar to make the bird fly and is attempting to avoid the pipes. If they hit a pipe, the pipes turn red to indicate they've been hit. You can view my code for the project by clicking on "View Repository".

2. 20-Time Project

During Grade 12 I worked on a 20-Time Project, or in other words a project that I dedicate 20% of my time to. I chose to create a soccer injury website that provides information on how to prevent and recover from various injuries. This is a subject that I am very passionate about because after playing soccer at a provincial level I have had my fair share of injuries. I wanted to share my knowledge on how to recover with others so that they can stay in the game. I used a website called WIX to create my webpage from scratch. I also used JavaScript to code a calendar that I plan on incorporating to demonstrate the different recovery timelines. This image shows the main page for injury prevention.
